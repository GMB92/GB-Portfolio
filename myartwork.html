UI/UX Design

Employer wanted to create a unique agile management tool that does not exist in the market. The team had to brainstorm a solution to create an application from scratch by creating the marketing brand, web design, and making it unique from competitors. I led the UI/UX portion of this project and was responsible for all design related decisions and deliverables. I collaborated with the product manager, engineers, and Subject Matter Experts to to design this agile management tool, keeping the end user in mind throughout the process. The software was implemented and introduced to the company and will be branched out to clients as well. Below is an example of an "edit" function.

A local nonprofit wanted to rebrand, modernize current website, and launch a new design to the organization in a tight timeframe. The organization had very limited technical expertise and availability to discuss requirements. As the lead UX/UI designer, I tackled this end-to-end redesign product by focusing on applying strategies from Human-Centered Design methodology. I was responsible for the accuracy and completion of all design-related work deliverables which included: conducting and synthesizing user research, building the wireframes, conducting usability testing, and building a prototype for the user interface. The client was thrilled about the work product and held an event to showcase the new design to the entire organization. Below is an example of the home screen that was shown at the event.
